Automated Theorem Proving Link to heading

There are 2 terms that have different meaning:

  1. Proof Assistant or Interactive Theorem Prover (wiki) – Assists with the development of formal proofs by human-machine collaboration. Usually in the form of keeping track of what you need to prove and what you have proved. For example: Coq, Lean, Agda, Isabelle, F*.
  2. Automated Theorem Prover – Uses AI (or other similar, relatively opaque algorithms) to come up with the steps to the proof.

General Properties Link to heading

  • Works in classical first-order logic (ru)
  • Follows automatic strategy but interactive
  • Prints trace of its rules as evidence

There is no practical automatic proof procedure, even for most complete theories. Even if there were, interaction with the machine would still be necessary for exploring mathematics.
